Why This Issue Is Important


According to N.A.S.A., these pieces of debris, flying through earth's low orbit, can reach speeds of up to 17,500 mph, which could have devastating effects on manned and un-manned space crafts in the event of a collision (2013). The current volume and rate of debris accumulating in low earth orbit is unsustainable, and poses a threat to current and future operations in orbit.

If operations carried out in orbit become impossible due to the rising amount of space debris, this could have drastic effects on the quality of our lives here on earth. Services such as GPS, Comunications, Remote Sensing, and many other scientific deployments in orbit are becoming more susceptable to the dangers of this debris, and what is known as the 'Kessler Effect'.

The Kessler Effect

The Kessler Effect predicts that the collisions between different peices of space debris will result in an exponential amount of both debris and other collisions. As each peice collides with eachother, they break apart into several more peices, all with different trajectories. Proposed Solutions

There are many proposed solutions for not only collision detection and armour shielding, but also in systems that can safely remove this debris all together.
