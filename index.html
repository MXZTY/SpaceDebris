Why This Issue Is Important

According to N.A.S.A., the speeds of these pieces of debris flying through earth's low orbit can reach up to 17,500 mph, which could have devastating effects on manned and un-manned space crafts in the event of a collision (2013). The current volume and rate of debris accumulating in low earth orbit is unsustainable, and poses a threat to current and future operations in orbit; however, this also affects the quality of our lives on earth. Services such as GPS, Comunications, Remote Sensing, and many other scientific deployments in orbit are becoming more susceptable to the dangers of this debris, and what is known as the 'Kessler Effect'.

The Kessler Effect

The Kessler Effect predicts that the collisions between different peices of space debris will result in an exponential amount of both debris and other collisions. As each peice collides with eachother, they break apart into several more peices, all with different trajectories.
